Tree, Tree, Tree

If you ask what I learn in these days, I can answer you in one word: tree. But I have to say, this jungle is fasinating.

Balanced Binary Search Tree

In my earlier blog, I referred to a binary tree of symmetry: balanced binary tree, or AVL. Every node of it has this feature: the difference between the hight of its left subtree and its right subtree is less or equal to 1.

AVL has some advantages: the time conplexity of its search action is O(logn). But there is some flaws, too. In the worst case, every time we insert a leaf or remove one will lead to the break of the restriction. Fix it takes time. The most naughty kid is deleting, he will make his parent unbalanced, but after our fixing that, its grandparent will loss balance. So we will spend logn time on adjusting the structure of the tree. That is not good.

Splay Tree

Splay tree is of intelligence! It will adjust itself based on the 20-80 rule. The most frequently used node will be raised to the top of the whole tree.

But it means well have to do adjustment every step. And if someone keep searching the deepest node(that is different node, for we raise the nodes every single time), the time complexity for a single move will reach O(n). The tree degenerate into a list.

It is the apropriate time for a superhero to save these coder! With a minor adjustment, the splay tree will be ‘smart’. That is, firstly adjust its grandparent’s structure instead of its parent’s. With this ‘leap’, the tree magically shrink in height into half of the previous. So, after enough times of searching, the splay tree will turn into an AVL and the time complexit of searching, inserting and deleting is O(logn)(progressively). Note: Every time we try to insert a node or delete one, we firstly raise its parent node(if it exists)(or just the last leaf our algorithm find)/itself to the root.

Bad luck may hit every one. One of the disadvantages of the splay tree is that the tree can hardly be perfectly balanced, so there may be one route that is very long(a node that is very deep). So we have to conclude that splay tree is not as stable as AVL.

Library! Where productivity reside!
